The cessation of microchip supplies to Russia by tech giants Intel and AMD could have significant and far-reaching consequences for the Russian populace. The immediate impact would likely be felt across industries that rely heavily on modern computing technology. This includes everything from personal computing and consumer electronics to more critical sectors such as healthcare, transportation, and communication infrastructure. In the absence of these essential components, the domestic production of technological goods would grind to a halt, leading to shortages and potentially driving up prices for consumers. Over time, this could stifle innovation and technological advancement within the country, leaving Russian tech companies struggling to compete on a global scale. The ripple effects could also extend to everyday life, potentially compromising the efficiency and effectiveness of services that people have come to rely on for their daily activities and livelihoods.
